being in a long distance relationship in 2026 means you're basically running a small remote company with two employees who are in love. and like any company, you've got a software budget, so let's audit it. every app we use, ranked by one question: **does this actually earn its money?**

## the free tier that carries the whole relationship

**Discord, free, and it's not close.** this is the actual home of our relationship. calls that stay open for hours, screen share for "watch me play," activities for built-in games, a private server that's basically our shared apartment except it's digital and the rent is $0. we tried Nitro for the better streaming quality, verdict: nice, not necessary. the free tier does 95% of the job.

honestly if you're doing LDR without a dedicated discord server for just you two, start there. it's the closest thing to leaving the door open between two houses.

## the "worth it" column

**Crunchyroll, worth it, IF anime night is a ritual.** here's the test: is anime a *scheduled thing* you do together, or a "sometimes" thing? if you've got a weekly episode night, the sub pays for itself in structure alone, a standing date that survives timezones because it's the same 25 minutes wherever you are. one account, fan tier, shared login, done. if anime is occasional for you, the free-with-ads tier honestly covers it.

**Spotify Duo/Family, sneaky good.** shared playlists are the most underrated LDR tool there is. we have a playlist that's basically a conversation. blend playlists tell you what your person listened to at 3am, which is either romantic or concerning depending on the week.

## the "audit this one" column

**Netflix, the honest answer is: it depends on whether you actually watch it *together*.** since the password-sharing crackdown, two households usually means two subs or the extra-member fee, so the maths changed. our test: if it's not hosting at least one watch-together night a week, it's not an LDR expense, it's just a personal expense you're both paying twice. we downgraded to the ads tier and put the difference in the moving fund. no regrets.

**watch-together tools:** Teleparty and co. are fine, but real talk, discord screen share with decent quality killed most of our need for dedicated watch-party apps. one person hosts, one watches, you both live-react in voice. free.

the flight fund. every app above, we judge by the same rule: **anything that isn't earning its place gets cancelled, and the money goes to the "same city" fund.** a couple of trimmed subs is ~$30/month.

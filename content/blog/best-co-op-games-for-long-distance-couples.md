real talk: "we should call more" doesn't work. calls with no *thing* to do turn into two tired people saying "so… how was your day" until someone falls asleep on camera. what actually worked for us was giving the call a job — and that job is co-op games.

after way too many hours of testing (for science, obviously), here's the tier list.

## the S tier — built for exactly this

**It Takes Two.** yeah it's the obvious one, but it's the obvious one for a reason — it's a co-op game *about a couple*, and here's the part people miss: you only need to buy it **once**. it has a Friend's Pass, so one of you buys the game and the other joins free. it's ~10 hours of "wait, YOU pull the lever while I— no not that lever" and it's the closest thing to being on the couch together that we found.

> pip's take: a game about a couple learning to cooperate, played by a couple learning to cooperate. the devs knew what they were doing.

> 🐦 5/5 | the LDR co-op gold standard. one purchase, ten hours, zero regrets.

**Split Fiction.** same studio (Hazelight), same one-copy Friend's Pass deal, and honestly? even better. you play two writers trapped in each other's stories, so one level you're both in a fantasy world, next you're in sci-fi. if you finished It Takes Two and had the "what now" sadness, this is what now.

> 🐦 5/5 | it takes two but more. the "what now" cure.

## the A tier — the long haul games

**Stardew Valley.** the ultimate "we're just existing together" game. no pressure, no timer, just two people farming and arguing about whether the money should go on sprinklers or a barn. we've had entire calls where we barely spoke and it still felt like quality time. that's the magic — it's *parallel* time together, which is the thing long distance steals from you.

**Valheim / Core Keeper / Palworld.** pick your flavour of "build a little home together in a dangerous world." there's something lowkey therapeutic about building a base with your partner when you can't build an actual home yet. we know. we're in therapy about it. (jokes. mostly.)

## the "this will test your relationship" tier

**Overcooked 2.** listen. this game has ended stronger couples than us. but if you can survive kitchen 4-2 without someone leaving the call, you can survive a visa application. genuinely recommend it *because* it's stressful — you learn how you two argue, and long distance means you need to be good at arguing over voice with no body language. Overcooked is training.

> ⚠️ **do not** play overcooked the same week as a visa deadline, a flight booking, or any conversation containing the phrase "we need to talk about money." trust us on this one.

> 🐦 3/5 | two birds deducted for emotional damage. still recommend.

## the practical bits nobody tells you

- **timezone the sessions.** we're Adelaide ⇄ overseas, so "evening" is one person's 6am. shorter sessions, more often, beats one big weekend binge someone's zombie for.
- **one of you having a potato PC is fine** — Stardew and Core Keeper run on anything. save It Takes Two for whoever has the better rig hosting.
- **Steam Remote Play Together** (one person owns, streams to the other) *exists* but over international distance the latency is rough. Friend's Pass games > remote play, every time.
- **discord stays open the whole time.**

> ❝ the game is the excuse. the call is the point.

## the actual point

every one of these games cost less than one flight to see each other. that's the maths that got us: date night infrastructure is *cheap*. it's the moving-in-together part that's expensive — and that one we can also help you with. run your numbers when you're ready.

Short answer: there's no single number, and anyone who gives you one is selling something.

Long answer: the *right* number for you comes from three buckets, and once you see them separately, the whole thing stops feeling impossible.

## Bucket 1 — the move itself

This is the one-off cost of physically getting you both into the same home:

- Bond or deposit, plus rent up front
- The relocation — flights, shipping, the visa if there's a border
- Basic setup: a bed, a table, the stuff two households don't magically merge into one

This bucket is big but *finite*. It has a top. You can save toward it.

## Bucket 2 — the in-between buffer

This is the sneaky one. If one of you moves before they can work in the new place — waiting on a visa, or just on a job — someone covers two people's living costs on one income for a while.

Add up your monthly costs together, multiply by the number of months you might be a one-income household, and *that's* the buffer. For a lot of long-distance couples, this bucket is bigger than the move itself.

## Bucket 3 — the "don't wipe yourselves out" reserve

Never spend your last dollar closing the distance. You want a normal emergency fund left standing on the other side — three months of expenses is a sane floor. Starting your life together financially winded is how a beautiful decision turns into a stressful one.
